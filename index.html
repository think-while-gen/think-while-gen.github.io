Interleaving Textual Reasoning throughout Visual Generation

Thinking-while-Generating (TWIG) interleaves textual thoughts with visual generation steps for on-the-fly guidance.

Abstract

Recent advances in visual generation have increasingly explored the integration of reasoning capabilities. However, existing methods typically incorporate textual reasoning either before generation (as pre-planning) or after (as post-refinement).

We introduce Thinking-while-Generating (TWIG), an interleaved framework that enables co-evolving textual reasoning throughout the visual generation process.

The TWIG Framework

Our framework interleaves textual reasoning with visual generation through three core schemes:

When to Think

Decoupling generation into controllable sub-tasks (visual regions) with a reasoning schedule.

What to Say

Generating textual thoughts as localized sub-prompts conditioned on previous visual content.

How to Refine

Conducting immediate "reflection" to critique and re-generate regions if necessary.

TWIG-50K Dataset

To support supervised fine-tuning, we curated TWIG-50K, a high-quality dataset containing 50,000 samples. The dataset is decomposed into thinking, generation, and reflection tasks to provide structured supervision.
